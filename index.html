Bridging the Digital Divide Through ICT Education

Education local students digital inequality and promoting ICT skills for equal learning opportunities

Why this Matters

Digital inequality means some students do not have equal access to technology and the internet. This can limit their learning and future opportunities. ICT skills are important for education, communication, and careers in today’s digital world. By raising awareness and promoting ICT education, we can help all students gain the skills they need and create equal learning opportunities. This also supports the growth of a smarter, more inclusive, and sustainable city.

Equal Opportunities for All


Teaching ICT in Myanmar government schools helps give every student a fair chance to succeed, no matter their background. Students in rural or low-income areas can access the same information and learning resources as others.

This reduces the gap between different communities and supports inclusive education. When everyone has equal opportunities to learn digital skills, more people can contribute to society and the development of a sustainable smart city.
